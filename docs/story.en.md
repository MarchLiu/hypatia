[Hypatia](https://github.com/MarchLiu/hypatia) was never a spur-of-the-moment project. Many years ago I was already trying to design systems that could apply broadly to dynamic information structures. More than a decade ago, when I was still at Kingsoft, I wrote a radical fully dynamic database application called *socrates*. That project performed terribly. I never gave up afterward—when I founded my own company I tried to build a document system with dynamic structure; later, PostgreSQL’s recursive queries helped me take a large step forward in this space. Reliable graph search became possible. That, in turn, led to my time at Huobi, where I built a search system over the full Bitcoin transaction network.


After that, as I finished my work on combinator design and published *The Construction and Implementation of a Micro LISP Interpreter*, another piece of the puzzle fell into place for my efforts in this field.


Thanks to the rise of modern AI, I was pointed toward directions I had never imagined. Through a graph query system I can give AI determinism, while AI’s general competence can unlock enormous value from that knowledge.


So this year’s [JSE](https://github.com/MarchLiu/jse/) became the technical basis for giving AI graph query capabilities, and I can define different knowledge bases for AI at multiple levels.


Before Hypatia, I built for my employer a centralized, large-scale knowledge graph aimed at hundreds-of-millions-scale data. After importing millions of foundational facts, it visibly expanded what AI could do, and AI’s ability to proactively build graph searches and explore knowledge far exceeded my expectations.


In some ways Hypatia is a scaled-down relative of that large central database; in other ways it is a very different work—small, compact, focused on local stewardship of knowledge and its dynamic creation, forgetting, and even mutual learning. I hope it can give individual users a controllable, lightweight memory service.


When I first thought about naming the project, I considered Laozi or his given name Li Er, but I wanted to reserve the name of one of China’s greatest philosophers for a grander theme. I even thought of *King Lear* as a homophonic joke—but I felt neither English speakers nor Chinese readers would easily catch the pun. So I asked AI to help me recall famous librarians in history. Until the name Hypatia returned to me. Her life is such a heartbreaking story that I could no longer pass over the name. I decided to dedicate this project to the last librarian of the Great Library of Alexandria, the last glow of the Greek schools, a great scholar history should remember—Hypatia. May she rest in peace.
